# Foozi – Homepage UX Specification

Version: 1.0
Status: Approved Concept
Owner: Product / UX
Last Updated: 2026-03-13

---

# Objective

The homepage is designed to serve three primary user intents:

1. **Check today's football matches**
2. **Discover insights and predictions**
3. **Explore teams, leagues, and statistics**

The page should feel **data-driven, modern and sports focused**, clearly differentiating Foozi from traditional livescore platforms.

Positioning:

> "Football Intelligence Platform"

Not:

> "Simple Livescore Website"

---

# Homepage Information Architecture

Order of sections:

1. Today’s Matches (Hero)
2. Tactra Insights – Top Facts of the Day
3. Community Predictions
4. Trending Teams
5. AI Match Insights
6. League Navigation
7. Tomorrow Highlights

---

# 1. Hero Section – Today’s Matches

Purpose:

Immediate relevance. Users land on the page primarily to see **today’s important matches**.

Content:

* Date
* Top matches of the day
* Match cards

Displayed Matches:

Top **5–8 matches only**

Full list available via:

"View All Matches Today"

Match Card Components:

* Home team
* Away team
* Team logos
* Match time
* League
* Tactra prediction score
* Key stat preview

Example Card:

Bayern vs Dortmund
18:30 — Bundesliga

Tactra Score
Home Win: 64%

Preview Stats:

* xG Trend
* Form Score
* Injury Impact

UX Rule:

Avoid displaying the entire match list above the fold.

---

# 2. Tactra Insights – Top Facts of the Day

Purpose:

Differentiate Foozi from standard football data websites.

Content Type:

Short data-driven insights generated from statistics and AI.

Example Cards:

Bayern Dominance
12 home matches unbeaten

Liverpool Attack
2.8 average xG per game

Inter Defense
Only 0.6 goals conceded

Card Layout:

Headline
Stat or insight
Optional link to match page

UX Rule:

Maximum **3–5 insight cards**

---

# 3. Community Picks

Purpose:

Gamification and engagement.

Content:

Prediction distribution from users.

Example:

Arsenal vs Liverpool
78% predict Arsenal win

Barcelona vs Sevilla
64% predict Barcelona win

Action:

CTA Button:

"Make your prediction"

---

# 4. Trending Teams

Purpose:

Encourage exploration and support SEO entry points.

Display:

Top 5–8 teams currently trending based on:

* match interest
* user predictions
* search traffic
* recent performance

Example:

🔥 Bayern
🔥 Arsenal
🔥 Inter
🔥 Real Madrid

Each entry links to:

Team page

---

# 5. AI Match Insights

Purpose:

Showcase Foozi's intelligence layer.

Content:

Short automated insights about upcoming matches.

Example:

Arsenal vs Liverpool

Arsenal scored in 7 of the last 9 home games vs Liverpool.
Liverpool won the last 3 away matches.

CTA:

"View Full Match Analysis"

---

# 6. League Navigation

Purpose:

Fast navigation and SEO structure.

Displayed Leagues:

* Bundesliga
* Premier League
* La Liga
* Serie A
* Champions League

Each item links to:

League overview page.

---

# 7. Tomorrow Highlights

Purpose:

Users often plan ahead.

Content:

Upcoming important matches for the next day.

Example:

PSG vs Monaco
Real Madrid vs Valencia

CTA:

"View Tomorrow's Matches"

---

# UX Design Principles

Key guidelines for the homepage:

Clarity
The page must immediately communicate today's football relevance.

Hierarchy
Important matches first. Data insights second.

Brevity
Avoid large statistical tables on the homepage.

Exploration
Provide easy entry points to teams, matches, and leagues.

---

# Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Do not:

* Display all matches of all leagues immediately
* Replicate a traditional livescore layout
* Overload the hero section with statistics
